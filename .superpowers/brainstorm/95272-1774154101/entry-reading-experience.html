How should a single monthly entry read?

Each entry has 2–5 named sections + a bullet list of random observations. Which reading experience fits?

A — Editorial / Magazine

Each section is a distinct named chapter with its own heading. You scroll through the full entry linearly. Sections separated by ruled lines or visual breaks. The bullet "Other random things" list gets a distinct treatment (looser, handwritten-feel). Immersive long-read.

B — Accordion / Field Notes

Sections are collapsed by default. Reader taps to expand each one. Feels more like field notes or a dossier — you choose what to read. The entry as a whole feels denser, more scannable. Good for quick revisits.

C — Issue / Publication

Each monthly entry is framed as an "issue" of a publication (e.g. Issue 016). A grid/bento layout shows sections as articles in a mini front-page. Each section can be clicked into as a standalone piece. Feels most like a real publication.

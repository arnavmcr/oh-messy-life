# March ‘25

### What is this and why am I doing this?

Hello. This is a monthly newsletter update thingi that I’d like to do to stay more connected with my friends. It is also my attempt at vulnerability, which I’m working on. If you are reading this, you are important to me and our friendship is something I value. Thank you for taking the time out.

This will also not be good writing, but it will be simple and unnuanced.

Also - feel free to reply/not reply at your own pace/convenience. The goal with this is not to force conversation or engage. It is to asynchronously, honestly and (within reasonable bounds) meaningfully remain “in touch” or connected with people I care about.

### In defence of a bucket-list

Watching Green Day was an out-of-body experience. They are a band that defined much of my childhood and adolescence, and to share this moment with people who felt all the angst and poetic-ness that defines the band made this a crucial chapter in a larger coming-of-age.

I outgrew Green Day in college, around the time I found the hipster and the cool. At at time when you could really be anyone, I forgot Green Day as I did supporting Chelsea, or listening to My Chemical Romance or any of the things essential to my childhood and late schooling years. The world outside was edgier and more accessible and less obvious -- why would you listen to Green Day on your iPod touch when you could go for a grimy ugly punk gig in your city? Why would you watch Chelsea on TV when you could watch Mumbai FC get whacked 4-0 in every game, sing along with a bunch of other fans?

It is more real and perhaps more fulfilling to engage with the world directly, immediately next to you. It gave me a vague sense of meaning and helped "build community" as they say, because it was mine and it was in front of me and its smallness made me feel like it meant more. But watching Green Day, ruptured my brain in a much more spectacular, limbic way. It was magnificent. It was childlike. It was real.

The world in front of me is still exciting and "cool" in the way Green Day isn't really, or hasn't been for many many years. Nor can I say that watching Green Day has suddenly jolted me into action and inspired me in the way that going for a small, local punk gig would. Yet, if anything and within reasonable bounds, watching Green Day, or going to Stamford Bridge or doing any of the other seemingly dumb things I wanted to do before my pre-frontal cortex had developed, are now things I am going to try do more of. Even if it's easier to forget or outgrow or cleanse myself of these things, I'd rather not.

### SeeSaw

Sometimes maybe good, sometimes maybe shit is how I now respond when someone asks me about Putt One or how things are generally. The numbness and avoidance that Bangalore gave me seems to have given way to some feeling, sometimes maybe good, sometimes maybe shit. Even if my journal word-map (ChatGPT generated ;)) springs up "Putt One" and "anxious" as the most frequently used words, I'll take it. This back-and-forth with myself is not something I'm trying to fight, and I'm going to try and let it take its course instead of shaping it.

### AI-yo technology

Using ChatGPT has provided many hits of somewhat well-deserved dopamine this month. I've long enjoyed both coding and it's ugly cousin no-code tools, because it allows you to create something from nothing, and enjoyed the painstaking process of actually doing it. ChatGPT does take away a lot of the "enjoying the process" and building craft and all of that stuff, but it has given me back more in sheer delight of speed and accuracy and ease. It is forced to not just indulge all my intrusive thoughts and nonsensical ideas but actualise them, mostly well. It is intoxicating. Maybe I've been blue-pilled (or red-pilled idk i haven't seen the movie), but it has so far allowed me divert energy to things I would like to and think about things more deeply and quicker.

# July & August ‘25

### What is this and why am I doing this?

Hello. This is a monthly newsletter update thingi that I’d like to do to stay more connected with my friends. It is also my attempt at vulnerability, which I’m working on. If you are reading this, you are important to me and our friendship is something I value. Thank you for taking the time out.

This will also not be good writing, but it will be simple and unnuanced.

Also - feel free to reply/not reply at your own pace/convenience. The goal with this is not to force conversation or engage. It is to asynchronously, honestly and (within reasonable bounds) meaningfully remain “in touch” or connected with people I care about.

### Let me be me

In the first ever edition of this newsletter, I wrote --

Mag Fields (and Echoes of Earth, for that matter) were made up of non-memories - no pretense, no hipster showing off about knowing every artist and every set, no rules. Both these festivals were returns to how and why I got into the music scene in the first place. Before the arrogance and one-upmanship became necessary to defend my position as a scene elder, there was an awe to going for gigs that Mag Fields helped me rediscover.

My trip to Goa was a reminder of all the many joys of dance music that are easy to forget when in Bombay (or Bangalore), and the even purer joy of doing for the first time, and knowing you will do it again. It was a watershed weekend (as Mag Fields, or my weekend in London were): the kind that separates your life into two sections, before and after. Dancing till noon-ish, mostly anonymous and mostly sober, this weekend continued my journey into discovering presentness, and what expression means to me.

On this trip, I learnt that it had been 10 years since "Talk to Me You'll Understand" by Ross From Friends released. I will not say I heard the song 10 years ago (that is a stretch for even my hipster-ness), but 7-8 years ago, hearing that song for the first time, left a similarly deep impression on how I view expression and art in general. Life and music seemed much larger and less rigid after I watched this song being played live (on YouTube) -- it felt like there were limitless possibilities and no rules as long as you were bold enough to be yourself.

I guess seeking out these moments (songs, nights, texts, smiles) that come to define the next version of yourself is ineffective (seek not what shall find you or wtv), but remembering them puts into perspective how much you've grown / changed since.

Small aside on the song linked with this WhatsApp message and title of this section: I heard this track being played from the carpark, around 6:30a. After eating a Snickers and smoking a cigarette, we tried to wade through the mucky road to listen to it, but to no avail. I guess this song (with its one line of lyrics, also the name of the song) exemplifies the point this section is trying to make. Of the hundreds of times I've heard this song (before and after Goa), this is now inexorably linked to that gloomy sunrise.

### Unknown return date

Big pillars and parts of my life in Bombay (and generally) have (or are leaving) for their Masters degrees with unknown return dates. Beyond all the pride and excitement of being a spectator to their new lives, I am keen to see the visceral version of life outside India.

Memories of my carefree yet character-defining year at Zilingo have essentially been glossed over by the pandemic, and since then, I haven't actively visualised life beyond Bombay and Bangalore (and Goa lol). Financially and intuitively, life in Bombay ticks all boxes, a (largely) stable, fulfilling and fun life. I've not really tested the logic of living in Bombay/India, it's just been something I decided as not large enough of a factor in my life design. But after these close-ups of life elsewhere, maybe it is something to reconsider.

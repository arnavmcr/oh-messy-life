# May ‘24

### What is this and why am I doing this?

Hello. This is a monthly newsletter update thingi that I’d like to do to stay more connected with my friends. It is also my attempt at vulnerability, which I’m working on. If you are reading this, you are important to me and our friendship is something I value. Thank you for taking the time out.

This will also not be good writing, but it will be simple and unnuanced.

Also - feel free to reply/not reply at your own pace/convenience. The goal with this is not to force conversation or engage. It is to asynchronously, honestly and (within reasonable bounds) meaningfully remain “in touch” or connected with people I care about.

#### Why this is late

Writing this month’s newsletter thingi was not easy - I have felt a gradual loss of control over my time. This is likely because I have been generally avoidant and have procrastinated on things that are important to me with the excuse that “I haven’t been in the right headspace”. This is true, yet May and June thus far have seen me lose more control over doing things to bring me back into said headspace. So in very “Eat, Pray, Love” fashion, I am going on a long vacation ![😊](<Base64-Image-Removed>) Less to find myself and more to lose some parts of myself that I have been far too connected to.

#### Days are short and years are also short

“Five years pass as five years do” has quickly become the starting point for my pale blue sunset introspection. Increasingly, I am reminded of things that happened a long time ago quantified in years - it is 10 years since I joined Xavier’s and met a lot of people who are reading this, 4 years since koi started and 5 years since my first Boiler Room. This calculus is frightening more so once you are reminded that this feeling is not finite - that this pace is, for the foreseeable future, unrelenting. It has seeped into my day to day.

I’ve found counting weeks to be completely futile and hence propose new markers of tracking time - pub quizzes, ecstasy pills, Cubbon Park runs, Hinge reinstalls, fuel tanks, tobacco packets, visa appointments, Splitwise dues et al.

#### How far we’ve come, how far left to go

This is something a very dear friend says (he will accuse me of stealing it and getting it tattooed so I am giving full semi-public credit to you Rahul). May (and June) allowed me to resurface the purest set of feelings I know - the pride, inspiredness, happiness and awe of seeing your homies succeed. Whether this be in side-hustles or on Strava or creative outlets or going to college or some odd combination of all, watching the homies come this far yet being acutely aware that there is so much left to be done is reassuring and inspiring. Thank you ![💓](<Base64-Image-Removed>)​

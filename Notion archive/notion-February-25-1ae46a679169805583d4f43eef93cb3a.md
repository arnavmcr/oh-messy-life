# February ‘25

### What is this and why am I doing this?

Hello. This is a monthly newsletter update thingi that I’d like to do to stay more connected with my friends. It is also my attempt at vulnerability, which I’m working on. If you are reading this, you are important to me and our friendship is something I value. Thank you for taking the time out.

This will also not be good writing, but it will be simple and unnuanced.

Also - feel free to reply/not reply at your own pace/convenience. The goal with this is not to force conversation or engage. It is to asynchronously, honestly and (within reasonable bounds) meaningfully remain “in touch” or connected with people I care about.

### A long goodbye

Exactly three years ago, I moved to Bangalore, filled with a sense of who I was, and directionally, what I wanted out of the city. To take this techbro pipe dream as far as it could possibly go, as quickly as possible. The security that my job provided, coupled with post-pandemic exuberance made for a very clear purpose, going into the city. So much so that a few months into the city, I wrote a manifesto that governed my time there, you can read it [here](https://tidal-seahorse-bc0.notion.site/1ae46a67916980dcb3abca72c8dda696?pvs=25). The vanity of this exercise aside, I had a far clearer sense of self 3 years ago than I do today.

Living in Bangalore was like an extended brunch: drunk off disposable income, coddled by suburbia and liminal, a hazy wonderland where rules truly do not apply. For every pound of flesh Bangalore took, it gave back tenfold in startup kool-aid. This kool-aid was more a sedative, dulling and numbing. I found myself distanced from former versions of myself much more frequently than ever before.

Mostly, I gained an avoidance that I am finding difficult to unlearn. Perhaps not fully attributable to my time in the city, this avoidance and numbness has become second nature in Bangalore where I was barely responsible for things -- anonymous and untethered. That is a large part of why writing this month's newsletter was harder than most months. Confronting what this long goodbye really means -- that I no longer have this haven where I could go to, where all else feels distant and immaterial, has been harder than I thought it would be.

### Again again again and on and on and on

Part of the Bangalore hangover, my 2022 manifesto, at its core, was anxious. It prescribed a frantic, fast way of life to avoid "life from catching up to me". That was fundamental to my worldview, to do as much as possible as quickly as possible.

The fear of irreversability and urgency is as stark as ever, but I guess logically and mathematically, I am wrong. Especially when it comes to my professional life, there is far more time ahead of me than behind. And that Putt One's future is not as fatalistic as I think it is, and the opportunity cost of choosing this path should not be calculated on a daily/monthly basis (as I do now), but over a much more secular period. Which is also scary, but removes the pressure of immediate "success". And the constant guilt that this opportunity cost instils is necessary in spurts, but largely unhelpful and anxiety-inducing.

To allow for compounding -- in knowledge, relationships, money etc -- is something to be learned and internalised, something I am not used to.

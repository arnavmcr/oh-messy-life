# October and November ‘25

### What is this and why am I doing this?

Hello. This is a monthly newsletter update thingi that I’d like to do to stay more connected with my friends. It is also my attempt at vulnerability, which I’m working on. If you are reading this, you are important to me and our friendship is something I value. Thank you for taking the time out.

This will also not be good writing, but it will be simple and unnuanced.

Also - feel free to reply/not reply at your own pace/convenience. The goal with this is not to force conversation or engage. It is to asynchronously, honestly and (within reasonable bounds) meaningfully remain “in touch” or connected with people I care about.

Two Tuesdays ago I found out that I got into INSEAD. Ever since, I have felt more lost, avoidant and anxious than I was earlier. Much of this year has been unmotivated and an internal struggle to do things, especially with Putt One. I spent most days staring out of my window, watching sunsets or chainsmoking, trying to muster up the will to do the obvious and basic tasks I needed to propel Putt One further.

Worse than lack of momentum or lack of 'success', this apathy came from a (self diagnosed) lack of motivation. Most decisions in my career unto this point were with 'entrepreneurship' as an endpoint, the final realisation and coalescence of the skills I value and a lifestyle I wanted. The foundational maxim of entrepreneurship (or anything to the same level of devotion) is one's unrelenting personal belief in what is being built, or the ‘problem’ being solved. I didn't believe that this rule applied to me when I started Putt One -- I didn't think I needed to love it all that much, that I could keep it at an arms length and decouple meaning and money. I was wrong. The 'grand narratives' of Scaler (real tangible changes to how people are educated), or After EOD (pushing the music scene to reflect more, even if at a limited scale) were essential to my motivation and excitement, things I didn't think I needed and were rules that didn't apply to me (my unrelenting belief in my own exceptionalism LOL).

So I expected this INSEAD news to wash over the pain and confusion of this past year. To some extent it has -- it allows me to reallocate time and energy away from Putt One and into spaces and projects that would solve for my apathy and move it closer to some kind of motivation. It is embarassing to 'shut down' Putt One and perhaps even more jarring to have come out of my first full-time entrepreneurial attempt more disillusioned and jaded than I was going in, but needs must -- sunk cost fallacy etc etc.

I guess validation from big business school ![™️](<Base64-Image-Removed>) hasn't really been the painkiller I was hoping it would be. The hedonic treadmill still marches on. The temptation/compulsion to continue applying (miss u HBS), and/or to fill the next 8 months with internships, gigs, travel, friendship, projects and ~networking~ has forced me to recluse myself for a few days at a secret Bangalore Airbnb (where I am writing this from), hoping for some panacea to emerge, instead I have been sleeping for 12 hours and watching the Office.

Not all is doom and gloom however -- I am happy to have the opportunity to be back in my favourite part of the world (Southeast Asia) and also close to my friends and family in the first-world (Europe). Yet in this moment, the grief of my vision of my own career (entrepreneurship) outweighs most excitement.

#### Other random things

My fantasy to fill the next 8 months is/was to do several two-week long projects that I would otherwise not have the time to do.

For example, spend two weeks as a day-trader, spend another two weeks on a trek, two weeks building out the personal CRM I have wanted to, two weeks learning how to edit/shoot videos etc etc.

I doubt this fantasy will come into fruition, but if you have any suggestions on two-week projects I can do (No bar on topic, field, vibe. The more random the better I guess) -- I am all ears :)

Playing sports is so so amazing. Also how on earth have I been sleeping on badminton for so many years?? Goated sport

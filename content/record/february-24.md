### What is this and why am I doing this?

Hello. This is a monthly newsletter update thingi that I’d like to do to stay more connected with my friends. It is also my attempt at vulnerability, which I’m working on. If you are reading this, you are important to me and our friendship is something I value. Thank you for taking the time out.

This will also not be good writing, but it will be simple and unnuanced.

Also - feel free to reply/not reply at your own pace/convenience. The goal with this is not to force conversation or engage. It is to asynchronously, honestly and (within reasonable bounds) meaningfully remain “in touch” or connected with people I care about.

### Lowlights

#### Individualisation of loneliness

After the crutches of situationships and MBA applications, I found myself having to confront the feeling of being “lonely”. It’s been a nagging realisation that combatting loneliness in your mid 20s is a function of effort, something I didn’t recognise or accept.

While loneliness is not an uncommon feeling, especially in Bangalore (the loneliness epidemic etc etc), this month forced me to individualise this feeling and examine it more deeply. Usual truisms like “put yourself out there” that I had disregarded as platitudes became things I put a concerted effort into doing, despite how anxiety-inducing and draining they were.

Looking at loneliness as something that is within or outside your locus of control has been helpful, yet challenging perceptions of myself (especially in social situations) is probably going to be a longer thread.

#### Stopping and starting and stopping

February was a jagged month, with travel and health punctuating what was an otherwise healthy, solid routine. It felt like I would take two steps forward, and one step back - a solid work week followed by being incapacitated and bed-ridden for a few days, or a healthy week exercise-wise followed by a weekend of debauchery.

Part of this jaggedness is short-sightedness on my part, for instance not stretching before a torturous spin class, or not hydrating while on ecstasy, it is in some part to do with my immunity, which is frustrating as it breaks momentum. My solution has been to start eating chawanprash.

I have felt this even within days, where there are tasks piling up, and then downtime, and the context switching ends up draining me significantly. I am looking for “reset mechanisms” that I can use through the day, to help clear my mind and get back on track. I’m trying to use the 5-min mindfulness thing on my phone, or going for little walks, but in the absence of cigarettes, this is not easy haha.

### Highlights

#### The return of intuition

Intuitive decision-making - doing what feels right - is how I made most decisions until last year, where I deferred strongly to what seems right. This past month I have felt this intuition gradually revive and guide my outlook toward decisions. Rebuilding this intuition was intentional, and took place through reflection and gathering of more information.

The big decision this has helped guide is to do with work. Last month I wrote about debating the more logical route of joining a VC versus starting up, and I’m excited to have chosen the latter as the path forward for me. This decision has been so freeing.

It is daunting to leave behind a job that pays an inordinate sum of money to a 25 year-old, but in the words of one of my colleagues, “I expected to fail in much bigger ways by this age”. To put myself in a situation where I can fail meaningfully, sans steady paycheck, sans fancy CV, is tremendously exciting, and offsets the concern.

On what I’m likely to build, there is a longer thesis that stems from my “non-fungibility”, and therefore feels like an extension of myself, rather than a departure. I will send this note (dramatically titled “The Collapse of Culture”) as a non-scheduled feature when it is ready.

#### Becoming my mother

As an elder daughter, I am becoming my mother. Even beyond the fact that I have started running and sharing workouts with colleagues and friends and making ridiculously leafy salads for dinner and all the obvious markers of my transformation into my mum, I’m learning the value of innocuous things she does and says.

Things like “use both hands” when carrying objects around the house, which I have extrapolated to patience and focus when doing things. Or her ability to delay gratification, but when it does come, she meaningfully immerses herself into the realisation of the gratification, like a lazy Sunday afternoon after a long run.

#### The habit of doing

With the near-term objective of starting up, the bias for action I alluded to last month is something that I needed to cultivate across the board. “Just say yes” to new experiences, and new people has been the principle I’ve tried to inculcate this month.

Some of these have been smaller “yesses”, to go to a club even though I had an early start the next day, or to pick up the guitar and record a shitty mopey love song. Some have been slightly larger commitments, like [After EOD](http://aftereod.substack.com/), where Rahul and I “examine alternative music cultures in India through a critical lens combining theory, ethnography and social commentary”.

### Other random things

I have significantly reduced my consumption of cigarettes, on most days not smoking in office. But I still thoroughly enjoy a morning rollie with a cup of coffee from my new second-hand coffee machine. This is the best impulse purchase (3k from some shady WhatsApp group) that I have made in a long while, and it is the perfect way to start the day.

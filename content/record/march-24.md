### What is this and why am I doing this?

Hello. This is a monthly newsletter update thingi that I’d like to do to stay more connected with my friends. It is also my attempt at vulnerability, which I’m working on. If you are reading this, you are important to me and our friendship is something I value. Thank you for taking the time out.

This will also not be good writing, but it will be simple and unnuanced.

Also - feel free to reply/not reply at your own pace/convenience. The goal with this is not to force conversation or engage. It is to asynchronously, honestly and (within reasonable bounds) meaningfully remain “in touch” or connected with people I care about.

### Systems check

March is difficult to characterise into lowlights and highlights - it was a month where I tried to build systems that would last into the chaos that I expect over the next few months. Systems with physical health - cardio, diet, strength, mental health with therapy and meditation and journaling. I am trying to get the basics of these systems right before transitioning into starting up full-time, as that will overextend me in many ways. Resetting to systems that work and serve me well will be crucial.

### The unease of doing business

In March, I ran a small pilot for my mini-golf business - designing and building the course, buying golfing equipment and testing out the game itself.

The physical build-out of the course was surprisingly smooth and easy (don’t tell anyone pls). From drawing it out to then identifying a contractor, purchasing materials and finally getting the items delivered, there were close to no hiccups in the process. Pretty much all of the above could have happened purely over WhatsApp - with different vendors and contractors independently co-ordinating with each other and ensuring that the build is on track and within budget.

However - the unease of starting up is beginning to kick in. Committing to a specific business model, putting myself out there and transmitting confidence are all threads that need to be tied in, sooner, rather than later.

Nonetheless - the pride of designing something from scratch and seeing it materialise into a physical thing, and playing it with your friends is a great source of motivation and reassurance as I move into the more formal parts of this venture.

### Same-same but different

The contents of good conversation have evolved only slightly, those having these conversations have evolved tremendously. Specifically when meeting old friends, those you have known and spoken to for close to a decade now, I have found that the topics of conversation are largely identical to what they were two, five or ten years ago. Yet the conversations feel far more authentic, as there is a greater sense of vulnerability to them.

Speaking about the same failed romantic encounter from many years before retains its pathos and humour, but is now layered with deeper reflection and discussion on patterns, life design and preferences. This opening up and the freedom to articulate and engage without judgement has made the navigation and processing of “growing up” a lot less lonely.

### When the sun hits

I have spent the last few days of this month in Gokarna where my arc with the beach has finally been settled. After spending my childhood despising the beach, to flirting with the concept during my time at Zilingo, this trip crowned and legitimised this rocky relationship. I have finally understood the appeal of floating in the water and watching the tide roll in and out endlessly, of people-watching sandcastle makers and pre-wedding photoshoots.

It was also an uncommon feeling for me to return from a holiday with net-neutral or net-positive energy, and this was perhaps the first time I’ve consciously and actively rested.

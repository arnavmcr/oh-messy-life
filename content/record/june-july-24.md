### What is this and why am I doing this?

Hello. This is a monthly newsletter update thingi that I’d like to do to stay more connected with my friends. It is also my attempt at vulnerability, which I’m working on. If you are reading this, you are important to me and our friendship is something I value. Thank you for taking the time out.

This will also not be good writing, but it will be simple and unnuanced.

Also - feel free to reply/not reply at your own pace/convenience. The goal with this is not to force conversation or engage. It is to asynchronously, honestly and (within reasonable bounds) meaningfully remain “in touch” or connected with people I care about.

### [In Undertow](https://open.spotify.com/track/6Px7VvXLTjMP6J2nTQ6bIh?si=4c6752973d844ac1)

After two months of adventuring where I turned 26, left my job at Scaler after ~4 years and travelled a bunch, I find myself in undertow - there is a sharp current trying to pull me into the future, but an equal force very much immobilising me. I expected to return from this vacation to a blank slate, it has been far from that - I have come back to an unresolved life that has finally crept up on me.

The current is simple to explain - to get to work on an idea I am confident in and passionate about, and to build a life in Bangalore. The force is harder to explain but its symptoms are straightforward - frightening cholesterol levels, several unopened WhatsApp chats, a near-absent parasympathetic nervous system and chronic fatigue.

Bringing these markers of physical and mental well-being to some sort of equilibrium, or building a life raft, is my current focus before I let this current sweep me away.

### [Home, like no place is there](https://open.spotify.com/album/172GEyILIzsGA8PJqrsHmL?si=6GuNaqhnQ2unVTfYekXA0g)

June and July have helped me reframe my understanding of "home." To see my friends in their adopted homes taught me the nuances of building a home - secure representations of themselves, away from their professions or programs that are typical markers of identity under capitalism. It was only because of their home building that I was able to truly immerse myself into the places I visited as their homes provided such a safe space to then sprinkle on top adventures and masti.

![](https://tidal-seahorse-bc0.notion.site/image/https%3A%2F%2Fprod-files-secure.s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com%2F743a16a0-8cd9-4648-bd76-d789f79e3cf6%2Fc84b9db1-b462-4936-b139-d3d575a66577%2Fe203474a-b819-4126-ab72-64e7992a89fa.png?table=block&id=226a9181-0bc1-4503-8fa4-b91ef54cb459&spaceId=743a16a0-8cd9-4648-bd76-d789f79e3cf6&width=450&userId=&cache=v2)

Some of these were literal - pinpoint directions to the nearest alcohol shop (and what to buy once there), instructions on how and when to close windows and blinds, and garbage-throwing cycles. All of these reduced the cognitive load of travelling. More subtle ways of home building were the 30-second download I would get before meeting one of their friends, lore on the neighbours, the buffet of activities, fun facts about innocuous buildings we would pass by and a play-by-play of how to buy drugs. These raw narrations allowed me a window into their lives, lives I have at some point imagined for myself. That they have built such homes in strange lands was inspiring and reassuring - each of them pursued a life inherently close to their heart and have come out on the other side better for it.

This is strange to write but I am applying these home-building principles to my extended stay in Bombay (until I return to Bangalore). It is almost like I have forgotten what the city is like to live in for more than a weekend - where you can make friends, do normal things like go to a shop and buy groceries, where you can exercise and function as a full person, not a weekend hotel resident as I have used it for the past couple of years.

### [The summer ends](https://open.spotify.com/track/0g7fjsXZNVVhKhrg2Jp9DK?si=26b5985e0112467a)

Exactly 5 years ago, I wrote this as part of a piece on my time in South/South East Asia -

> But perhaps the soundtrack of that month (July 2019) and the ones that followed was one of both regret and longing. Two bands that dominated my listening were Alvvays and American Football, both of whom played in Jakarta in and around the time I was there – neither of whom I saw. In this quest toward finding meaning and disassociating from appearance, live music, for the most part, is a step in the right direction. This awareness made my absence at these gigs that much more unfortunate, and has also ensured that the opening notes on the classic “Never Meant” evoke specific images of Jakarta’s skyline.

(the full thing is [here](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1OwayQik8mXEfiS6FSmVpI3oB6PSsB13Mjget7c1glpk/edit?usp=sharing) in case you would like to read it, it has aged better than I expected it to)

This June, this somewhat prophetic paragraph came full circle - I saw both Alvvays and American Football in the span of about a week in the UK. This is not isolated - the past couple of months have felt like everything is coming full circle, all the time, everywhere, always.

![](https://tidal-seahorse-bc0.notion.site/image/https%3A%2F%2Fprod-files-secure.s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com%2F743a16a0-8cd9-4648-bd76-d789f79e3cf6%2Fa007dda3-15db-46f9-acd8-19c96adf93c5%2F4F237807-7044-4B51-81C4-59F776549281_1_201_a.jpeg?table=block&id=79e4db56-54be-4944-94f1-868ee559c4fb&spaceId=743a16a0-8cd9-4648-bd76-d789f79e3cf6&width=770&userId=&cache=v2)

This has been overwhelming for me - the feeling of finite urgent compulsion to "do everything now" because it feels like it brings something full circle. Anxiety-inducing questions around doing things before they end, such as "When is the next time I am going to see this friend" or "If I don't watch this band, I'll never get a chance to" exacerbate this compulsion, an accelerated and extrapolated strain of capitalist realism. There is an apocalyptic feeling of finiteness which forced me into short-term, anxious consumption. To put it much more crudely, it is being a part of some "rat race" and seeking gratification before the opportunity to gratify is deleted.

This is a representation of a dysregulated nervous system that is only now learning to zoom out and breathe, that living fast and dying young is not the only mantra and that maybe the world is not ending every day.

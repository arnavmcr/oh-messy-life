### What is this and why am I doing this?

Hello. This is a monthly newsletter update thingi that I’d like to do to stay more connected with my friends. It is also my attempt at vulnerability, which I’m working on. If you are reading this, you are important to me and our friendship is something I value. Thank you for taking the time out.

This will also not be good writing, but it will be simple and unnuanced.

Also - feel free to reply/not reply at your own pace/convenience. The goal with this is not to force conversation or engage. It is to asynchronously, honestly and (within reasonable bounds) meaningfully remain “in touch” or connected with people I care about.

### Lowlights

#### Being uninspired, constrained and “bias-for-action”

Many of this month’s lowlights are interconnected and stem from a larger feeling of being uninspired. Since college, I’ve always had “things to do” along my primary focus: koi, college festivals, MBA applications, which were a great way to learn new things and direct energy (both markers of feeling inspired IMO). Of late, I have not found a lot of things that I consider “worthy” of my energy, and this entitlement is something I am trying to work on.

My Scaler job is ridiculously well-paying, and that prices me out of side-projects by colouring the way I view things - as a return on investment on my time, and that too very monetarily. I feel jaded and money-oriented, which has resulted in me being constrained to pursue things that would normally inspire me.

This has also distanced me from something that I consider a strong characteristic of mine - “bias for action” (a word I throw around to office juniors a lot). The zest of just doing things, sometimes not thinking, but throwing yourself into situations that are novel and inspiring is something I held as a core tenet of my being. This has waned, with my uninspiredness and transactional view on life contributing.

### Highlights

#### Living slowly and sometimes fast

I spent this month living slowly, again, something I am unused to. I took time to learn how to cook new things with patience and attention to detail, went for walks to a nearby lake, cleaned my room and wrote a lot. Things that I felt like I knew would help me get in touch with myself, but that I usually disregarded as platitudes and was troubled by the long time it takes before these feel satisfying.

To my surprise, things like soaking chickpeas overnight so you can make hummus is rewarding and do help with building comfort with delayed gratification. I have begun looking at these activities as intrinsic to my day and things to look forward to, rather than slots on my calendar or, in the case of exercise, a thing to do while watching TV. I tried to balance this with some masti (to preserve this whole bias for action thing) - Control Alt Delete was a weekend of almost no sleep and so much chaos. That weekend in Bombay had all the youthful exuberance I now reminisce about - from smoking a joint in the KRUNK office, bitching about Kalaghoda Cafe and being starstruck when meeting the Lightyears Explode at Control Alt Delete.

#### Falling in love with Obsidian again

PKMS - personal knowledge management systems - is something I have been obsessed with since forever, and after a long hiatus, I’ve gotten back into it. Going through old notes was such a fun exercise and a reminder of how much has changed and what a crazy time your mid-20s is.

I’ve always known about the compounding power of note-taking and the past month has been a reconnection to this.

This also led to something tangible - I wrote semi-formally about music after many years. You can read the piece [here](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1We0zg4lGoM4LzSayKoyat3uqngZeg-Uru3VMO8X4ImU/edit?usp=sharing) (opens in a different tab). The entire process of going through old notes, forming a point of view, researching, writing and finally sending it out was so fulfilling and I hope to continue doing this.

#### The goodness in people

For professional reasons, I’ve reached out and spoken to many new people this month, and each of them has been supportive, understanding and empathetic. I was overwhelmed by the effort that people took to understand my background, share insight and (almost unconditionally) offer help where they could.

Perhaps in my jaded, transactional and material point of view, I forgot that this is generally how people are and it’s motivated me to be more vulnerable and open with new/old people.

### Next steps

I am considering two primary paths forward - moving into investing or starting a business. The logical answer is investing, the more intuitive one is starting a business. As someone who has taken decisions primarily on intuition (that is risk-adjusted with logic), and whose decisions have largely paid off, taking a more logical decision and committing to investing requires some kind of rewiring, which I’m not super comfortable with.

Over the past couple of years, I have felt my risk appetite drop significantly, and move towards “safer” options that “appear better”. I expect that my risk appetite will continue to fall, so it feels like there is no time like the present to start up from a personal lens.

Investing seems like bells and whistles with few solid skillsets to pick up, but from the outside in, does seem like a layover and something I’ll go into with one foot in and out.

On what I’d like to start - there are broad strokes. I know I’d like to build a “dhandha” and not a “startup”, I am bullish on offline businesses and not tech, and I am also certain I want to build for urban, upper-middle-class India.

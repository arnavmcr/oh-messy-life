### What is this and why am I doing this?

Hello. This is a monthly newsletter update thingi that I’d like to do to stay more connected with my friends. It is also my attempt at vulnerability, which I’m working on. If you are reading this, you are important to me and our friendship is something I value. Thank you for taking the time out.

This will also not be good writing, but it will be simple and unnuanced.

Also - feel free to reply/not reply at your own pace/convenience. The goal with this is not to force conversation or engage. It is to asynchronously, honestly and (within reasonable bounds) meaningfully remain “in touch” or connected with people I care about.

### Putt yourself out there

November and December-ish saw Putt One meet the real world. It felt like it was long overdue, and that it finally marked something of a starting point. That I had finally reached day 0, and this is where things start. From the perspective of the business, it is true -- this is where the business-y things start of making money and all that comes with that. I did not anticipate how much this threshold is also true for my brain to be stimulated -- it was something like night and day. Finally, I am able to have fun problems to solve. This is nourishing and of course stressful, but it feels, also, long overdue and welcome.

### HSR Payout

The anxiety of Putt One starting out is also correlated with a recognition that it has been 6 months since I haven't received stable, regular income, and that at some soon date, I will need to become more frugal. The effect of this frugality will perhaps first mean that I will have to stop renting my house in Bangalore, unless a very strong reason to continue renting comes up.

I forget how deeply attached I am to that house, and Bangalore as a city. It was easy to forget, swept away in the intensity of Bombay and a rationalisation to ensure I don't backslide, but coming here for half a week was all it took to remind me. It felt swansong-ish, packing boxes and reminiscing.

Perhaps it was the emotional intensity of the half-week or the drugs, but Echoes of Earth was the best possible psuedo-ending to my life in Bangalore. It was a very special weekend and will remained etched in my brain for a long while. It joins a host of dear images including getting a cigarette in my balcony after playing hours of FIFA, sunsets from the old Scaler office and the view from above my Brio's steering wheel. Continuing the theme of frenetic full circle moments, the first time I visited Bangalore alone was to go for Echoes of Earth.

### Et tu

Processing people letting you down is not something I am used to. Individual personal failures or deviation from expected outcome are significantly easier to process and come to terms with. I have been somewhat fortunate (or uncaring) to have not had to deal with someone else "betraying" me, not that it is as dramatic as it sounds, but the feeling of eroding trust due to specific misaligned instances is what I am considering as betrayal.

Perhaps it truly is not all that deep, that giving the benefit of the doubt to the other party is in fact healthy and kind of a zen way of looking at things. But this is hard to digest, as it requires me to disengage and remain "stoic", a dangerous, slippery slope to become a completely uncaring, jaded and oblivious person. Vocalisation and engagement in this discomfiting is necessary to cross a certain threshold in any relationship, but in interpersonal relationships, it feels more draining than before.

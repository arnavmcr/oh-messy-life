### What is this and why am I doing this?

Hello. This is a monthly newsletter update thingi that I’d like to do to stay more connected with my friends. It is also my attempt at vulnerability, which I’m working on. If you are reading this, you are important to me and our friendship is something I value. Thank you for taking the time out.

This will also not be good writing, but it will be simple and unnuanced.

Also - feel free to reply/not reply at your own pace/convenience. The goal with this is not to force conversation or engage. It is to asynchronously, honestly and (within reasonable bounds) meaningfully remain “in touch” or connected with people I care about.

### Just keep running

Just keep running was the refrain of an essay I once wrote, something Aleya reminded me of the night before the run. It is a paraphrasing of my mum’s general view toward life and running marathons and seems like a fitting title for this little section on running the half marathon. After much will-he-won’t-he, I ran the half marathon and enjoyed every second of it. I finally decided to run after I realised that it starts at 5a, meaning that it gets over by 8a, usually the time around which I wake up, meaning that it’s a question of just waking up early one day and just doing it. This framing was incredibly helpful and made it seem like much less of a commitment.

The run itself was scarily easy — no injuries, very little stopping and a lot of fun. A large part of that day was just orchestration, as I have written earlier, and mixing the right hormonal cocktail. All the caffeine (coffee and 1-2 of those caffeinated gels), and some nicotine had created some wonderous cortisol-adrenaline balance, my playlist and the route had endless amounts of serotonin and dopamine for the moments I needed it most.

### Dhandha mode

I don’t want this newsletter thing to become only about Putt One/starting up, so I am doing a separate more business-y monthly newsletter for Putt One. The objective is slightly different, it is to help me define and create focus, as well as to serve as some kind of accountability for me. Let me know if you’d like to be added to that too — I’m sending the first one out tomo!

Putt One made some actual hard cash in January, a feeling I had forgotten and perhaps the hardest-earned money in my whole life. Until the Lil Flea (the first large monetized event. if you don’t know what the Lil Flea is, just imagine all your Instagram ads in one giant venue), revenue was numbers on a screen, now that I have actually made money, I have been thrust into far more existential crises than before I did. Actual revenue means actual comparisons, depressing arithmetic when compared to the comfort of a salaried account. In all my libertarianism, revenue is a very naked number, one that is cold and hard and real, and is perhaps the most crucial determinant of your business and its value, a very sad realisation, given that until this point it was all vibes and coolness.

I guess this is all in line with delaying gratification and enjoying the process and my love of the game, but it’s now all very real. There are actual stakes and with that, lots of fun and lots to be done. Every month feels like you’re at the starting line all over again, and that is very humbling.

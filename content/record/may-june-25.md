### What is this and why am I doing this?

Hello. This is a monthly newsletter update thingi that I’d like to do to stay more connected with my friends. It is also my attempt at vulnerability, which I’m working on. If you are reading this, you are important to me and our friendship is something I value. Thank you for taking the time out.

This will also not be good writing, but it will be simple and unnuanced.

Also - feel free to reply/not reply at your own pace/convenience. The goal with this is not to force conversation or engage. It is to asynchronously, honestly and (within reasonable bounds) meaningfully remain “in touch” or connected with people I care about.

### Overcorrection

This is two-ish weeks late (by far the most overdue of all the 17 times i’ve done this) because I'm trying to ensure that everything I write on this is fully honest. Not directionally honest, or somewhat honest or sometimes honest, but actually honest.

At times, it has felt like this little newsletter thingi has become a performance, a portrayal of control over my own life to the people around me. This direction has been self-created, a self-imposed deadline for monthly epiphanies or realisations or progress meant to be celebrated or lamented.

It is becoming clearer to me that I actually have no idea what I'm doing.

I have spent a lot of my adult life overcorrecting -- reacting to stimuli (usually “the mainstream”) with disgust, rejecting it and running as far and as fast from it as I can, retrofitting logic to this decision. This is quite an easy way to live life. By placing artificial constraints -- first logical, then material and finally moral -- on oneself, it's easy to keep running within the narrow space you've afforded yourself. It is fulfilling as well, to live to negate and to react. I'd go as far as to say that it is laudable. To live to disprove, with the predilection like that of a fucking alternate hypothesis. But it's starting to feel dishonest and lazy to live in this zero-sum fashion, where you surrender to these constraints.

So as a fully new experience for me in my whole life, I am actually trying to first figure out what I'm solving for before setting off on some wild goose chase to try and make it happen. Maybe I will continue to do Putt One. Maybe I will apply to business school with my tail between my legs, maybe I will bite my tongue and venture back into the deep, dark startup job market. Maybe I will pursue my long standing dream of becoming a professional sports gambler...

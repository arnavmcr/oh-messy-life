### What is this and why am I doing this?

Hello. This is a monthly newsletter update thingi that I’d like to do to stay more connected with my friends. It is also my attempt at vulnerability, which I’m working on. If you are reading this, you are important to me and our friendship is something I value. Thank you for taking the time out.

This will also not be good writing, but it will be simple and unnuanced.

Also - feel free to reply/not reply at your own pace/convenience. The goal with this is not to force conversation or engage. It is to asynchronously, honestly and (within reasonable bounds) meaningfully remain “in touch” or connected with people I care about.

### The world has turned and left me here

Google Photo memories reminded me that I had my HBS interview on 30th October one year ago. A year on, I have come to peace with the outcome of that seemingly fateful day. I found myself on a family vacation in Conoor where somehow my cigarette glowed warmer and more halogen-y than I'd ever seen and the midwest emo playlist was pushing me to relinquish urbanity for a life in the mountains, finally realising that where I am right now is where I wanted to be. Starting a kitschy fun business is what I've wanted to do for a long time, and in all likelihood, post MBA, I would have found myself exactly where I am. Obviously, much better equipped and better networked and with the illusion of a future-proofed career, but these are things I don't really value in any case.

In the year that has passed since, there was an overwhelming feeling of the world having turned and having left me here, that not going to school was somehow the end of the world. It was left largely unprocessed and haunting, as I tried to brush it under the carpet and distract myself; but finally, it appears to have been exorcised.

### NIRP

(yes, I watched Succession this month)

I often (and still do) deride people as "not real". It is a snooty thing to say about people, calling them "not real" or "lame" for being unidimensional or uninteresting. This is typically used on dating apps or at parties, where I labelled those I met as cookie-cutter versions of each other, usually because they would consume a different culture than I. This is silly -- everyone is a NIRP (not a real person) and everyone is an RP (real person). I found/find it easy to hierarchise and stratify people on these meaningless markers of personality, yet this process is mostly meaningless when constructing personality is so easy. And that even in resisting the construction of personality, you are constructing personality.

The aestheticisation or "core"-ification of everyone and everything makes everyone part of an endless cycle of signalling and personality-making. I thought I was above this, and I had some moral high ground to classify someone as a NIRP, but I am not and that is ok. This has been a particularly hard thing to digest, to disavow myself from quick, gratifying judgement to some sense of equanimity when it comes to meeting new people and rewiring my perception of them.

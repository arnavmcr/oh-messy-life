### What is this and why am I doing this?

Hello. This is a monthly newsletter update thingi that I’d like to do to stay more connected with my friends. It is also my attempt at vulnerability, which I’m working on. If you are reading this, you are important to me and our friendship is something I value. Thank you for taking the time out.

This will also not be good writing, but it will be simple and unnuanced.

Also - feel free to reply/not reply at your own pace/convenience. The goal with this is not to force conversation or engage. It is to asynchronously, honestly and (within reasonable bounds) meaningfully remain “in touch” or connected with people I care about.

## Highlights

### Music festivals and a return to innocence

This is a shorter version of a longer note that I scribbled into a notebook I bought at the airport about a week after Magnetic Fields.

Mag Fields (and Echoes of Earth, for that matter) were made up of non-memories - no pretense, no hipster showing off about knowing every artist and every set, no rules. Both these festivals were returns to how and why I got into the music scene in the first place. Before the arrogance and one-upmanship became necessary to defend my position as a scene elder, there was an awe to going for gigs that Mag Fields helped me rediscover.

Perhaps it was the drugs, the seeing-your-ex, the lack of sleep, the cold, the new/old friends or just the inevitability of the “four on the floor” (I hate this phrase) that rang through those 72 hours, Mag Fields was a return to innocence.

## Lowlights

### HBS & “waiting to exhale”

Both the HBS result and the weeks leading up to it had me holding my breath and putting life on pause. Putting situationships, phone calls, work, exercise and travel on pause. And when I found out I didn’t get in, more than anything, there was this dread of having to pick up the pieces of things that I had neglected, which was incredibly difficult.

The result itself was acute heartbreak and has forced me to face questions that I had conveniently shrugged under the carpet. Especially things like why am I doing the MBA, where do I want to live/work, and what kind of work I want to do. All of these were questions that I had excellent but not fully honest answers to, but now I’m forced to really grapple with them.

### [Anxiety, it’s all about me](https://open.spotify.com/track/3xvVuLA3YYZPd5mx8NOnN6?si=fa0b8fb08daa43fb) (the title is a song)

In the build-up to the HBS result and after (actually, for the better part of 2023), since there were so many things that went unprocessed and neglected, there was a lot of built-up anxious energy that manifested itself. From slipping back into old, bad habits to random outbursts, I was unable to cope with these feelings in the best way.

I’m glad that I am able to accept and share this, as it is something I’ve never really done openly, and now work on addressing it

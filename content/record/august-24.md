### What is this and why am I doing this?

Hello. This is a monthly newsletter update thingi that I’d like to do to stay more connected with my friends. It is also my attempt at vulnerability, which I’m working on. If you are reading this, you are important to me and our friendship is something I value. Thank you for taking the time out.

This will also not be good writing, but it will be simple and unnuanced.

Also - feel free to reply/not reply at your own pace/convenience. The goal with this is not to force conversation or engage. It is to asynchronously, honestly and (within reasonable bounds) meaningfully remain “in touch” or connected with people I care about.

### Quelle surprise

Expectedly but still contentiously, I will (likely) be based out of Bombay for the foreseeable future. This is because I have an "unfair advantage" to build this mini-golf business from Bombay. Both in terms of getting off the ground (vendors, contractors and space) as well as the things will keep this going (mental health, structure and routine).

In previous editions of this, I have said that I would be committing to Bangalore. Over the past few months, I have come to realise that I was trying to commit to a Bangalore that I had hoped to discover - hacker houses, a disregard for precedent, the whole move fast, break rules etc - the whole Silicon Valley charade. While I am sure it does exist, I am unsure of whether I want to be chasing this dream and stop vilifying myself for not wanting to chase it at this point.

In an ideal world, I would love to be split between the two cities and hopefully I’ll figure out a way to make that happen!!

It has felt like I'm living in Bombay for the first time as an adult (mostly true with the Zilingo travelling, the pandemic and then living in Bangalore), and it has been kind. Somehow travelling seems easier, making plans less cumbersome, cops much nicer, and humidity more tolerable.

Without over-romanticising Bombay (something that can be done endlessly), I have found its age inspiring. The literal pace of life here is at odds with overarching patience and respect, especially while building a "dhandha"-ish business. No smoke and mirrors, just a strong emphasis on getting things done, something I am trying to get into the rhythm of doing.

Unless something drastically changes - this business fails, I fall out with my family or I miss Sarjapur <3, I will spend this phase of my mid-20s living adult life in Bombay.

### What is this and why am I doing this?

Hello. This is a monthly newsletter update thingi that I’d like to do to stay more connected with my friends. It is also my attempt at vulnerability, which I’m working on. If you are reading this, you are important to me and our friendship is something I value. Thank you for taking the time out.

This will also not be good writing, but it will be simple and unnuanced.

Also - feel free to reply/not reply at your own pace/convenience. The goal with this is not to force conversation or engage. It is to asynchronously, honestly and (within reasonable bounds) meaningfully remain “in touch” or connected with people I care about.

### Lowlights

#### Panic! at the office

I resigned from my role as Associate Director of Strategy at Scaler on 5 April. My decision was pre-meditated (as is clear from past versions of this newsletter), but I, nor anyone else, could have predicted the drama and furore that unfolded in the five days that followed.

My resignation coincided with an unprecedentedly large restructuring exercise within the company, and within our team as well. Navigating this had many Entourage / Succession (I have been told, idk) style events - secret phone calls, code words, violating many data privacy laws, crying in my car, hardballing, and secret lists - all of which were exhilarating tests of character. These were cortisol and adrenaline-fueled micro-decisions that I felt like I had been trained for. This is fully some main character energy, but those five days were the most fun I had had in a long, long time. My play-by-play version of events, with each micro-event documented, would make for a great season finale if my former manager ever goes through with his idea of making a TV show about Scaler.

After things settled, in the weeks after, I found myself completely hollow. The rush of resigning and everything that transpired after gave way to acute sadness and emptiness. Realising the very painful implications of restructuring on colleagues I had worked with for many years, seeing the ethos of a company that has professionally raised me shatter overnight and feeling the weight of the aforementioned decisions has handicapped me.

_(Instagram post by @cigfluencer\_)_

Accepting and dealing with this acute burnout feels like it requires me to do something I’ve not done in a long time - “live serendipitously”, as my dear friend put it. To trust myself to take each day for what it is, and take the load off needing to have meticulous planning and action and structure that I am so used to and crave.

The implications of this temporary change in my approach to life have meant that my strides towards starting up have become smaller and slower until I am able to replenish mentally and physically. Other implications include a sharp increase in weed consumption and a massive improvement in my FIFA skills. Suggestions on how to be less high strung are most welcome 

### Highlights

#### Committing to Bangalore

April also came with the acceptance that Bangalore is not a layover, as I always imagined it to be, but somewhat of a destination. This has come with less comparison (especially to Bombay) and more effort to appreciate Bangalore and its quirks. Of its many features, like slow, clear and calm sunsets, or the crop of weekend BBQ stalls that pop up around my house, my favourite is the Thursday night exodus.

Driving around the Outer Ring Road after 8p on a Thursday is painstaking not because of the vehicular traffic, but the throngs of people lining the roads with suitcases, backpacks and bus tickets. Whether it is a strategically planned casual leave facilitated by an overnight bus to the countless “weekender” destinations, or a trip back home or something other still, this mini-migration is a distinctly suburban migrant Bangalore activity that I now see as a reminder of truly living in this city.

#### Flat and flatmates

Mine and most people who moved to Bangalore over the past few years’ first touchpoint with the city was the Flat and Flatmates Facebook group (why it is not flats and flatmates, idk). This chaotic forum harries you into finding a flat and flatmates that IMO is one of the top three determinants of your experience in the city.

Compounded also by the transitory nature of most residents, your flatmates are usually a group deal - with their SOs, their friends and their colleagues becoming extended tenants. These are confusing and precious relationships that typify Bangalore’s openness and blurred social fabric. Also, through most weekdays, travelling to see friends comes with many hoops to jump through, the proximity of these characters opens up the possibility of more spontaneous interactions.

These relationships break the monotony and insularity that one can easily slip into, and I am grateful for my flatmates, their friends, my friends and their flatmates, Sarjapur friends, running friends in Sarjapur, weed friends in Sarjapur, and my colleagues and their flatmates.

#### A run to remember

The next step in my evolution of becoming my mother lmao was running a “race” - the TCS World 10K. Besides all the usual stuff of running helping you reclaim agency over your own life, learning the true value of adrenaline and how to manage it and the meticulous orchestration of the day and night before the run, there were two things I actually enjoyed more than any other part: the first, smoking a fresh out of the pack ice-burst after it was done, and the second was planning what music to listen to while running.

I thought this run would be a good opportunity to put my DJ skills to the test, as well as calibrate my mental state through the race and match that with the music I would need at that stage. The end product was the perfect sequenced playlist to what was a fun and not that intense run.

Highlights include When the Sun Hits by Slowdive when the sun was really hitting as it rose across Ulsoor Lake, Greek Tragedy by the Wombats for the brief moments where it actually felt like I was on ecstasy (ya gay ik), and Bangarang towards the end.

The name Raxit Tewari is not just synonymous with the local music sub-culture; it is one that epitomizes it. In any of his roles, as the front man of the indie/electronic/rock act Sky Rabbit or the man behind the moniker Your Chin, Raxit’s vocals are a gateway to lush sooundscapes. At the Red Bull Tour Bus show in late 2013, while collecting our entry bands, far from the actual stage itself with only a muffled murmur audible, a fellow attendee told me with absolute confidence that Your Chin was performing. Baffled by the certainty with which she made this proclamation, she explained that there was only one voice in the music industry that sounded remotely like Raxit’s, with his raspy, matter-of-fact vocal style distinguishing him from the herd.



Peeping Till It’s Noise is Your Chin’s follow up to 2014’s Scatter Nature, which also had 4 tracks. Continuing the trend of minimal artwork that was evident on his previous work, the EP piques the listener’s curiosity even before the first synth patterns of ‘Fighting the Sumo’ are played. An out-of-sorts celebration sonically, the opening track traverses between a handful of chords. A jumpy, happy-go-lucky arrangement served with a healthy dosage of meaninglessly anthemic vocals, ‘Fighting the Sumo’ is one of the quicker songs within Your Chin’s repertoire. A head-bobbing, foot-stomping all-around party tune ‘Fighting the Sumo’ is laced with spacey keyboard chords and heavily processed guitar runs.

The track fades into ‘Hawk’, which opens with a slightly distorted, unsettling chord pattern. As the vocals come in, there is a clear departure from the jubilant, carefree sound of the preceding track. A more mature, dark sound with biting samples and subtle scratches, ‘Hawk’ follows the typical ‘Your Chin’ song pattern, with the drum and bass loops supplemented with occasional ‘one-shots’, adding diversity to the instrumental parts of the song. Parts of the verse see a faint synth following the vocals as they move around the octaves. The second segment of the song sees the piano make an entrance, with the extremely popular ‘cut’ vocals taking center stage. The frequency bending, processed vocal pattern resembling a vocoder and the break beat drum loop create an atmosphere of decay, a plea to curb abandonment of-sorts.

![18433-your-chin-main-i3](/images/peeping-till-its-noise-ep-review/18433-your-chin-main-i3.jpg)

Up next, ‘Shine’ begins with ominous, pounding kick-drums layered beneath long-drawn out, restrained yet incredibly powerful vocals. Lyrically ridden with rhetoric, the track is filled with a sense of yearning. There is a clear feeling of resignation in the vocals, which complements the down tempo, ambient nature of the music impeccably. An unobtrusive yet hard-hitting song, ‘Shine’ is an auditory depiction of weariness, loneliness and emptiness, another unique song for the set-list.

The closing song on the record, ‘Skit’, immerses the listener into an ethereal, synthesizer laden world, with a piercing kick-drum and addictive chord-pattern. The fantastically layered track, with claps, kicks and harmonies flooding, and melodious synth loops teasing the listener. The frivolous lyrics emanate an unusual innocence which the previous tracks were bereft of. Well-used breaks diversify the song, and while the chord sequence remains more or less the same through the song, the song only momentarily flirts with monotony.

Your Chin produces another solid record, cementing his status as indie music stalwart. While genre and labels do not even remotely dictate Your Chin’s music, it is abundantly clear that the music has moved into a more synthesized space. The synthesizers used were grimey and heavy, slightly reminiscent of Flume. The record also exposes the ‘dark’ side of Raxit, with mature, minimal and contemplative musical and lyrical arrangements being featured. ‘Peeping Till It’s Noise’ is an anthology of ‘urban love songs’, whose profoundness lies in their arbitrariness and nonsensicality.  The record is a gentle reminder that there is far more to Raxit than his ubiquitous smile and heartwarming voice.

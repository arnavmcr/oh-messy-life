**_Article about an organisation I work for that organises night-cycling rides. Original article can be found [here,](http://communitycode.co.in/20th-june-2016/) on the website of an NGO called CommunityCode I work for_**

In a world where days are spent frantically traversing from meetings, pushing and shoving to make the train and pulling all-nighters to squeeze out a modicum of sanity to write a college application, we tend to overlook the seemingly frivolous yet monumental parts of our life. Appreciating the finer, more subtle forms of happiness that do not include approval ratings, grades or promotions is a pivotal part of realizing ones potential and self-worth. An organization that ensures that its patrons take a fervent step towards attaining this self-actualization is ‘Mumbai Riders.’



Started deep into 2015 by three enterprising students, Mumbai Riders (MR) -has gained a cult fan-following over the past six months or so. “The idea was to bring the bicycle tour culture (from Europe) to Bombay. We thought, we’ve done it across the world, so why not in our own city?” says Rishi Shah, on how the genesis of MR took place, with co-founder Falak Parikh nodding in affirmation. All three of the founders had, at some point, indulged in cycling trips across continental Europe, often covering hundreds of kilometers in the process. The basic idea behind the organization is to hold bicycle rides through curated routes to allow people a unique insight into the city and its quirks. While bicycle tours are a novelty to the city, MR distinguishes itself from the run-of-the-mill organization in several ways.

 

First off, MR rides usually begin at midnight. The very thought of cycling through the night was, admittedly, ludicrous at first, but on further analysis it started to make a lot more sense. The sheer volume of vehicles and the sheer lack of civic sense amongst their drivers make cycling through the day a health hazard. Moreover, cycling at night is in itself a therapeutic experience. Without constantly worrying about vehicular threats, patrons can freely roam the empty streets, with the street lights and sea breeze replacing computer screens and sewage stench. Clichéd as it may sound; cycling across the city at unearthly hours erases all that is worrying and painful, even if it is only for a few hours. There is nothing more fulfilling than eating a samosa at 3am, watching the waves crash on the tripods on Marine Drive and sharing stories with other riders.

 

Secondly, MR have a curated, properly thought out and planned route, spanning from Colaba in the south to Worli or Bandra in the north. The route has been selected to give riders the opportunity to explore heritage sites all along the way. From the haunting, looming pillars of the Asiatic Library to the perennial symbol of Bombay the Gateway of India; riders are treated to once-in-a-lifetime views of structures that epitomize this city. The route also takes one through the picturesque Marine Drive, which is still bustling with life at 4am and to the idyllic Haji Ali Dargah. The organizers provide for enough and more breaks along the way to ensure that even the lowest common denominator is taken care of. The selection of this route unintentionally promotes the conservation of heritage sites, a pertinent socio-political issue that affects us all.

 

Most importantly, the team that organizes these events is incredibly hospitable yet firm when required. Their commitment to provide city-dwellers an escape from mundane daily grinds is laudable, as are their efforts to do this in an environmentally friendly manner. Well-informed, selfless and easy to talk to, the band of volunteers accompanying the riders create an environment of safety and comfort for riders across the spectrum. From the initial registration process, to the collection of refreshments and subsequently the ride, jokes are thrown around, bonds are forged and a general sense of camaraderie is developed.

 

What most believed to be a fad when started, MR has grown from strength to strength since their inception, not just in popularity. “Most of our rides sell-out in a few hours, it’s crazy that people actually wait on their computers just to book our tickets,” says Saurabh Punamiya, one of the founders, beaming from ear to ear. The fifty people group assembles themselves around eleven thirty, and the ride ends between three and four, depending on the speed of the rider. Proper safety measures have been put in place, with a car leading and tailing the group. With the choice of both geared and non-geared cycles, amateurs and dedicated cyclists’ needs are catered to. The organizers also have proper police permission in place for the rides.

 

Today’s generation faces a multitude of grave problems, with the advent of rapid globalization and imminent climate change being two of the most severe. Organizations such as MR can only grow in relevance from here on, and it is imperative that they do as they address these pertinent issues in an enjoyable manner. Our world and our city need more brainwaves such as this, if our generation is to stand tall to these worrying prospects.

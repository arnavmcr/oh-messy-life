When did our society progress (or regress) to this. How have we, even with our concepts of liberalisation and humanisation (go gay people!), reached a point where the need for the term ‘political correctness’ has come to exist.

The real question I would like to ask as of now is, what the fuck is political correctness. Another marvel of the 21st century, Wikipedia, describes it as _‘the t__erm used to criticize language, actions, or policies seen as being excessively calculated to not offend or disadvantage any particular group of people in society’_. And while this definition is undoubtedly one of the most politically correct definitions I have read, it leaves my head simmering with an ocean of questions, the most pertinent being what the fuck is political correctness, or more specifically what the fuck is political incorrectness.

We have sensitised ourselves to a point that we feel the need to care about things, not only around us, but all over the world. But not in the usual way. Us, or Humanity 2.0, is far too hipster to do things the old way. The new fangled method of care we have adopted is the Internet. The internet has allowed every human being to overnight become political scientists before elections in America, economists when Iran is sanctioned, sociologists when  beef is banned in Mumbai, social workers when Kim Jon Ung starves his people (or when China kills dogs) and most importantly, the internet has made every individual a fucking intellectual. It has given people, quite like myself, the illusion that firstly people actually read what the write and secondly that it will actually make a difference. We try so hard to care for others, we really really try. All of us try so hard that if you don’t try and if you don’t share the picture of dead Chinese dogs, you don’t care about humanity.

Coming back to the question, political correctness shouldn’t be a term. On one hand we call ourselves liberal(go gay people!) and then we create terms such as these that basically close of our mind to an entire section of thought processes that are far fewer in number. Take racism for example. By dismissing people’s legitimate arguments because they are ‘politically incorrect’, we are unknowingly contradicting the ‘open mindedness’ we pride ourselves on.
